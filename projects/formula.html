Olin Electric Motorsports

Leading the development of a high-performance accumulator system for Formula SAE Electric.

Role Accumulator Lead
Team Olin Electric Motorsports
Technologies SolidWorks, High Voltage Systems, FEA

Overview

Olin Electric Motorsports (OEM) is a student-run Formula SAE team that designs, builds, and races an all-electric, formula-style race car from scratch every year. As a member of the team, I have contributed to the mechanical and electrical integration of our vehicle's most critical system: the accumulator (battery pack).

Accumulator Lead

In my role as Accumulator Lead, I am responsible for the design, fabrication, and testing of the 600V battery pack. This involves managing a sub-team of engineers to ensure the system meets rigorous safety standards set by FSAE, while optimizing for weight and thermal performance.

Key responsibilities include overseeing the mechanical design of the container, cell module configuration, and ensuring seamless integration with the Battery Management System (BMS).

Design & Fabrication

The design process began with extensive CAD modeling in SolidWorks to determine the most efficient layout for the lithium-ion cells. I performed Finite Element Analysis (FEA) on the container structure to ensure it could withstand the required G-loads during competition.

Fabrication involved high-precision assembly of busbars and maintenance-plugs, alongside careful wiring of the high-voltage tractive system. Every step required strict adherence to safety protocols to prevent arcing and thermal runaway.

Testing & Competition

Before hitting the track, the accumulator undergoes rigorous testing, including isolation monitoring checks and full-load discharge cycles. At competition, our work is evaluated by industry experts through technical inspections and dynamic events like acceleration and endurance.
